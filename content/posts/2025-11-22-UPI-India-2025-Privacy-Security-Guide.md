## Enhanced Security Framework for 2025

### NPCI's New Information Security and Compliance Framework

The National Payments Corporation of India has rolled out comprehensive security measures:

**Key Components:**

1. **Zero Trust Architecture**

    - Never trust, always verify principle
    - Continuous authentication
    - Micro-segmentation of network access

2. **Mandatory Annual Security Audits**

    - Conducted by CERT-IN empanelled auditors
    - Complete vulnerability remediation required
    - Alignment with ISO 27001 and PCI DSS standards

3. **Stricter Account Linking**
    - More rigorous security checks when linking new bank accounts
    - Multi-factor verification
    - Additional identity confirmation steps

### Transaction Security Features

**UPI Transaction ID Standardization**

-   From February 1, 2025, only alphanumeric characters allowed in transaction IDs
-   Special characters will cause transaction decline
-   Enhanced system efficiency and security

**Limits on API Inquiries**
To prevent server overload and potential exploits:

-   **Balance checks:** Maximum 50 times per day
-   **Linked bank accounts viewing:** Maximum 25 times per day
-   **Transaction status refresh:** Maximum 3 times per transaction

**Autopay Regulations**

-   Recurring payments processed only during non-peak hours (before 10 AM and after 9:30 PM)
-   Limited retry attempts
-   Ensures system responsiveness during high-activity periods

## Privacy Measures You Need to Know

### Explicit User Consent (NPCI Guidelines 2025)

Under compliance with the **Digital Personal Data Protection Act**, NPCI now mandates explicit consent for:

1. **Balance Inquiries**: Apps must ask permission before checking your balance
2. **Listing Linked Accounts**: Explicit consent required to view your linked bank accounts
3. **Penny Drop API**: Account verification requires your approval
4. All API usages must be transparently communicated

**What This Means for You:**

-   Greater control over your financial data
-   Transparency about data usage
-   Ability to revoke permissions
-   Protection against unauthorized data access

### Deactivation of Inactive UPI IDs

To mitigate risks from dormant accounts:

-   UPI IDs inactive for over 1 year are automatically deactivated
-   Reduces chances of unauthorized access to old accounts
-   Protects against fraud using abandoned virtual addresses

**Action Required:**
If you have UPI IDs you're not using, either:

-   Use them at least once a year
-   Or accept their deactivation for security

### Regulation of "Collect Payments" Feature

Due to fraud concerns:

-   **Person-to-person collect payments** capped at ₹2,000
-   Feature limited to large, authenticated merchants
-   Reduces social engineering fraud
-   Prevents scammers from exploiting collect payment requests

### Mobile Number Update Requirements

Banks and Payment Service Providers must:

-   Update mobile number records weekly
-   Deactivate UPI IDs linked to dormant mobile numbers
-   Minimize transaction errors from expired/reused numbers

**For You:**
Always update your mobile number with your bank when you change it.

## Best Practices for Secure UPI Usage

### 1. Authentication Security

**Enable Multi-Factor Authentication:**

-   Use biometric locks (fingerprint/Face ID)
-   Set strongUPI PINs (avoid birthdays, sequential numbers)
-   Never share your UPI PIN with anyone
-   Change PINs periodically

**For Device Security:**

-   Keep your smartphone locked
-   Use strong device passwords/patterns
-   Enable remote wipe capabilities
-   Install security apps

### 2. App and Platform Safety

**Download from Official Sources:**

-   Use Google Play Store or Apple App Store only
-   Verify app publisher before installing
-   Check app reviews and ratings
-   Be wary of unofficial UPI apps

**Keep Apps Updated:**

-   Enable automatic updates for UPI apps
-   Install security patches promptly
-   Update your smartphone OS regularly

### 3. Transaction Vigilance

**Verify Before You Pay:**

-   Double-check recipient UPI IDs
-   Confirm transaction amounts
-   Review transaction details before UPI PIN entry
-   Be cautious with QR code payments

**Avoid Public Networks:**

-   Never use public Wi-Fi for UPI transactions
-   Use mobile data or secure home networks
-   Consider using a VPN for added security

### 4. Monitoring and Alerts

**Enable Transaction Alerts:**

-   SMS alerts for all transactions
-   Email notifications for large payments
-   Push notifications from UPI apps
-   Real-time balance updates

**Regular Statement Checks:**

-   Review bank statements weekly
-   Verify all UPI transactions
-   Report unauthorized transactions immediately
-   Keep transaction records for disputes

### 5. Protecting Personal Information

**Never Share:**

-   UPI PIN
-   OTPs (One-Time Passwords)
-   Card CVV
-   Bank account credentials
-   Aadhaar OTPs

**Beware of Phishing:**

-   Banks never ask for PINs via call/SMS
-   Don't click links in suspicious messages
-   Verify sender identity before responding
-   Report phishing attempts to your bank

## Common UPI Scams in 2025 and How to Avoid Them

### 1. Fake Payment Screenshots

**Scam:** Fraudsters send fake payment screenshots claiming they've paid you.

**Protection:**

-   Always verify in your UPI app
-   Check bank SMS alerts
-   Never hand over goods/services until money is in your account

### 2. Help Desk Scams

**Scam:** Callers posing as bank/UPI app support asking for OTPs or PINs.

**Protection:**

-   Never share OTPs with anyone
-   Hang up and call official bank numbers
-   Banks never ask for sensitive information

### 3. QR Code Replacement

**Scam:** Scammers replace merchant QR codes with their own.

**Protection:**

-   Verify the merchant name before paying
-   Confirm amount matches
-   Use merchant category code (MCC) verification

### 4. Refund Scams

**Scam:** Fake refund requests asking you to enter UPI PIN.

**Protection**:

-   Refunds never require your PIN
-   You can only receive money, not send during refunds
-   Verify refund sources through official channels

### 5. Collect Payment Fraud

**Scam:** Unauthorized collect payment requests.

**Protection:**

-   Verify sender before approving
-   Check amount carefully
-   Remember the ₹2,000 cap on P2P collect payments
